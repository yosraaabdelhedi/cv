Teaching Philosophy

Goals and Classroom Implementation

Assessment

Assessment is a fundamental in any subject teaching / learning that is required to enhance both the student’ s and the teacher’s performance. For, I apply varied assessment methods including:

  • Summative Assessment: using mid- and full- term tests to show growth and mastery of the four skills.
  • Formative Assessment: ongoing monitoring to evaluate student comprehension of the material.
  • Student Formative Self-Assessment: to enable the students to reflect on their own understanding of the lesson’s learning goal, find out whatever suits them best in terms of study skills and learning styles, and decide about their most urgent needs as a learner.

Motivation

Motivation is proven to have an impact on students’ levels of both effort to acquire a foreign language and desire to reach that language proficiency. For, implementing motivational strategies as well as a supportive teacher behaviour contribute to bringing positive results on students’ motivational belief, engagement, and learning achievement.

One of the major motivational strategies I utilize is to help the students identify their goals. As Tony Robbins claimed “Setting goals is the first step in turning the invisible into the visible.” Accordingly, the students need to be aware of the importance to learn and master the target language, which contributes to creating a kind of intrinsic and/or extrinsic motivation for them. As well, the teacher’s implementation of the appropriate materials and activities is a key to promote students’ motivation.

Active and student-centered learning

The instructor’s guidance and implementation of the appropriate activities are crucial to ensure that the students are actively engaged with the course material. For, I create natural learning conditions in the classroom and select various activities that provide opportunities for all learners to actively participate, meet the students’ needs, and help develop logical and critical thinking.

These activities include discussions, problem solving, case studies, role plays and other methods.

Project-based learning

It is another ingredient that boosts the students’ self-confidence and reliance. For, I always call my students to make projects which help them learn how to conduct research, plan, and execute their projects.

These projects include creating brochures and leaflets, PowerPoint presentations, school magazines, videos, etc.

Authenticity

Learners need to be aware that the target language is not only a subject matter, but it is also a means of communication of a large population in real-life situations, which contributes to positively impacting the learners’ interest and engagement. For, I use authentic materials that provide real-life examples of language used in everyday situations, and that can also provide information about the target culture. The materials I use include TV shows, online videos, Radio broadcasts, songs, magazines, letters and emails, news articles, brochures, websites, blogs, and social media posts.

The use of Technology

I believe the use of technology inside and outside the classroom Helps the students gain 21st-century technical skills necessary for future occupations. Accordingly, I always integrate the technological means available at work to engage students and provide them with easy-to-access information, accelerated learning, and fun opportunities to practice what they learn, work in collaborative activities, and practice the target language.

These activities include discussions, problem solving, case studies, role plays and other methods.
